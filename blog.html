Who was the first 'scientist' of the Earth?

Written between 07.10.20-...

Have you ever wandered, who was the first scientist of the world? To find a meaningful answer to this question, one needs to carefully conduct an investigation. And the investigation should start with asking the ultimate question; "who is a scientst?". In a sense, can one accept early homo sapiens who invented using tools, making use of fire etc. as scientists? Or does the essence of science lies within the concept of conformity: Can one became a scientist without satisfying their other earthly needs? Seneca suggested that in order to continue 'thinking' one must conform their earthly needs; i.e. one must be rich enough to 'think'. Today we all know that Seneca was filthy rich and spent his life in confort. So, what is the key difference between a scientist and an inventor? Who is the first inventor and/or scientist? When did homo sapiens decided to value science, or did they ever?
